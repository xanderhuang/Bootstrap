Into the Forgotten Forest Yosemite National Park

Following the trail of pebbles,
I found my way into the woods.
Passing by a forest of trees,
I got lost in the mists of darkness.
Guided by a ray of starlight,
I believed in destiny and serendipity.

Wounded by thorns and brambles along the way,
I struggled to navigate through the history of love.
Nearly fainted on the edge of a cliff,
My hands started to tremble.
But I knew if I fell you would catch me.
Taking a deep breath,
I was brave enough to take the leap.
Embracing the roseate sky,
I was ready to show my vulnerability.

Suddenly,
I was saved by a lone wolf.
And I knew I found my lost half.
Together we built a tree house in the forgotten land,
singing and dancing with joy and tears.
Freezing cold and raging fever in bed,
Till the velvet touch of your hands eases my pain,
Then I realized you have always been here.

Late at night, I heard you whisper in my ears.
Intertwined with your thoughts and feelings, I kissed you.
And you moved your tongue to make me moan.
We kissed and kissed,
until we were stoned on kisses.
Attracted to each other’s body scent,
We cuddled with our legs locked or crossed.
As daylight passed through the window,
I grinned for I saw us twisted in bedsheets.

I used to take everything you gave for granted.
From squandering to cherishing,
I shaped you, and you sketched me.
Because of you, I found myself.
Since that night in early November,
the seeds of yours have taken root in my garden.
And I’m covered in you.

Darmstadt Times

Love in the time of COVID-19

June 10, 2020 by Xander

By being an exchange student, I have cultivated a global perspective and felt in love with the city of Darmstadt. My journey as an exchange student to Technische Universität Darmstadt in 2020 was the highlight of my college career, both academically and culturally.

Onset of a Journey

Immersing myself in the aerial view of Frankfurt, I overlooked buildings and streets illuminated by the glow of city lights. My partners and I arrived in Germany at the break of dawn after the plane landed at Frankfurt Airport. Dragging luggage through miles of airport corridors, we eventually found the bus stop at terminal 1 and waited for the Airliner Bus to travel to Darmstadt. On arrival at the university, the Darmstadt host students led us to our shared flats (WG Zimmer) and took us on a sightseeing tour of the city. On the way to the heart of the city, I was captivated by the hustle and bustle of Luisenplatz. I took great delight in grocery shopping at ALDI SÜD, REWE, and Go Asia Supermarkt which are highly recommended for those who are coming to TU Darmstadt. Moreover, I was particularly fond of the fresh berries bought at a fruit stand in Luisenplatz because of the great hospitality of the street vendor.

Welcome to Darmstadt

The next day, the Darmstadt host students gave us a warm welcome along with a university brochure providing detailed instructions on how to pay our semester fee, take out health insurance, register courses online, and validate student ID card as well as the Athene card. During an icebreaker session, I had the opportunity to communicate with foreign students. This moment established the new path to mutual friendships. I have kept in touch, through different digital platforms, with some of my friends I made there as we forged a close bond of friendship. In addition, I started to take my one-month German intensive course where I developed and polished my German language skills. The class was composed of nearly fifty students from various ethnic backgrounds; the course was both demanding and fruitful because Magdalena was a passionate, enthusiastic, and supportive teacher who strived to provide a fertile setting that made learning engaging, enjoyable, and inclusive for all her students. I appreciate learning in such a stimulating and nurturing environment and spending some time chatting with Magdalena.

COVID-19 Outbreak

However, due to the worldwide spread of the coronavirus disease, TU Darmstadt canceled classes between March 15th and April 20th. Shocked and stunned, I was a bit depressed about this dramatic change in university policies. Within one week after the corona situation had deteriorated rapidly, most of my peers faced endless stress and decided to return to their home countries. On the contrary, I determined to stay in Darmstadt and complete my exchange program. From the application for a scholarship and the completion of graduation requirements to airline reservations, Germany visa application, and finding accommodation abroad, I had sacrificed a lot to prepare myself for this opportunity and would not give up halfway.

Quarantine

Not only did I watch Netflix and play games on Nintendo Switch during my self-quarantine, but I also spent more time associating with this splendid city. It was the time I became obsessed with the beauty of this city. I found myself entranced by the way the trams in Darmstadt are intricately interwoven with the city. In my view, they are the veins of this city that facilitate interactions between buses, passengers, and traffic.

Typically, I began my day with a bowl of breakfast cereal (Vitalis Knuspermüsli Schoko+Keks) with milk. Then, I improved my German through the mobile App, Busuu, which contributed remarkably to my learning progression in German. After that, I enjoyed cooking with my next-door neighbor, though my culinary skills are limited to boiling water and washing dishes. Fortunately, my neighbor is such a talented cook who is willing to spend considerable time in the kitchen preparing meals for his friends. We mingled with each other after dinner under the attraction of city lights and sometimes go out for nighttime shopping. As dusk fell, we spent the nocturnal hours on a stroll in the park which had become a regular leisure activity for us. I was pleased with the tranquility in which Nature was slumbering.

Cultural Experiences

In terms of socio-cultural aspects, I was glad that I have never experienced racial discrimination in Darmstadt, where a cultural melting pot has been constructed to maintain harmonious relations between the city’s ethnic groups. Furthermore, I recognized that Germans are more environmentally friendly and integrally connected with nature. For instance, they take their waste separation and recycling as well as the concept of greening seriously; they purchase bio-products as advocacy for organic agriculture, sowing the seeds of success to sustainable development. Perhaps owing to their efforts in environmental protection, I was fascinated by the fresh scent of green dewy grass after showers of rain. I could not help but indulge myself in inhaling this delicate fragrance on a rainy day. Additionally, the climate of Germany features a wide seasonal temperature variation and has four distinct seasons. Frozen and struck by an icy blast of air, I could not stand the chill in the air in winter. Nevertheless, as April approached, it began to thaw. The onset of spring carried the sense of rebirth, vitality, and flourish.

While the novel coronavirus outbreak was a hurricane ruining my exchange journey, panic buying of toilet paper amid coronavirus fears was such an intriguing social phenomenon. Not until the end of April was the toilet paper shortage resolved; I was so thrilled when I could ultimately buy a roll of toilet tissue. Before the COVID-19 pandemic, Germans’ reluctance to wear face masks resulted from their association of wearing a mask with a sign of a serious illness. Nonetheless, to weather the storm of COVID-19, the practice of wearing a mask has been common in Germany since face masks became mandatory in all shops and on public transport. Highly commendable were their adherence to social distancing policies and their commitment to the combat of coronavirus disease. In the middle of May, citizens of Hessen were allowed to join a gym, so I could finally perform resistance training and do aerobic exercise in Fitness Fabrik.

Courses

As for the courses I completed in Darmstadt, they are online seminars, taught via Zoom and Moodle, focusing on group discussions of a particular subject instead of emphasizing academic lectures. I was contented to use Catma for linguistic annotation and have fruitful discussions with Prof. Bartsch and Prof. Bender in the courses Annotation and Pragmatics as well as Computer Applications in Linguistics respectively. One of my research projects explored the social interaction between humans and machines, whereas the other investigated WHO Director-General’s public discourse amid coronavirus pandemic. Having several assignments to be completed throughout the whole semester, I faced the challenge of managing the struggle to meet the deadline and striking a balance between my studies and my leisure time. With a keen interest in travel and culture, I have traveled to Frankfurt, Mainz, Wiesbaden, Mannheim, etc., grabbing the opportunity to explore a foreign country and adapt to a new culture.

Inspiration

Late adolescence is a critical period of development; my semester abroad was an essential step in my university career that enabled me to develop an international outlook and gain cross-cultural experience. While the city of Darmstadt has created a sense of belonging, I am heading back to Taiwan.

Some of my peers are entering the workforce after graduation. Anxious about the opportunity to obtain employment, my classmates doubted their abilities to compete in the labor market since working in such a fast-changing environment full of uncertainties is like setting off on a voyage of discovery. I am glad to complete my exchange program which has inspired me to pursue a master’s degree in both linguistics and computer science. As Jeniffer Lee once said “Be fearless in the pursuit of what sets your soul on fire,” my abiding passion for computational linguistics is steeped in the notion that the application of computational methods has never been forced labor but an indispensable technology to improve people’s lives. I wish to learn and exploit the state-of-the-art computational techniques for the benefit of humanity, and I aspire to deepen my knowledge in the field of natural language processing and equip myself with the skills necessary for a job in this technological age.
